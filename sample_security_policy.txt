COMPANY INFORMATION SECURITY POLICY
Version 2.0 | Effective Date: January 2025

1. DATA ENCRYPTION POLICY

1.1 Data at Rest
All sensitive data stored on company systems must be encrypted using AES-256 or equivalent encryption standards. This includes:
- Customer personal information (PII)
- Financial records
- Healthcare data (PHI)
- Proprietary business information

1.2 Data in Transit
All data transmitted over networks must use TLS 1.2 or higher. HTTP connections are prohibited for transmitting sensitive information.

1.3 Encryption Key Management
- Encryption keys must be stored separately from encrypted data
- Keys must be rotated every 90 days
- Access to encryption keys requires multi-factor authentication

2. ACCESS CONTROL POLICY

2.1 User Authentication
- All users must use strong passwords (minimum 12 characters with complexity requirements)
- Multi-factor authentication (MFA) is mandatory for:
  * Administrative accounts
  * Remote access
  * Access to sensitive data systems

2.2 Privileged Access
- Administrative privileges must follow principle of least privilege
- Privileged accounts require annual review and approval
- All privileged access must be logged and monitored

2.3 Password Management
- Passwords must be changed every 90 days
- Password history: last 10 passwords cannot be reused
- Account lockout after 5 failed login attempts

3. INCIDENT RESPONSE POLICY

3.1 Incident Classification
Security incidents are classified as:
- Critical: Data breach, ransomware, system compromise
- High: Unauthorized access attempt, malware detection
- Medium: Policy violation, suspicious activity
- Low: Failed login attempts, minor security misconfiguration

3.2 Response Timeframes
- Critical incidents: Response within 1 hour, resolution target 4 hours
- High incidents: Response within 4 hours, resolution target 24 hours
- Medium incidents: Response within 24 hours, resolution target 72 hours
- Low incidents: Response within 72 hours, resolution target 1 week

3.3 Notification Requirements
- Critical incidents require immediate notification to CISO and executive management
- Data breaches must be reported to affected parties within 72 hours
- All incidents must be documented in the incident tracking system

4. VULNERABILITY MANAGEMENT POLICY

4.1 Vulnerability Scanning
- Internal vulnerability scans must be performed weekly
- External vulnerability scans must be performed monthly
- Critical and high vulnerabilities must be patched within 30 days
- Medium vulnerabilities must be patched within 90 days

4.2 Penetration Testing
- Annual penetration testing is required for all public-facing systems
- Results must be reviewed by security team and remediated within 60 days

5. DATA BACKUP AND RECOVERY POLICY

5.1 Backup Requirements
- Daily incremental backups of all critical systems
- Weekly full backups retained for 90 days
- Monthly backups retained for 7 years
- Backups must be encrypted and stored off-site

5.2 Recovery Testing
- Backup restoration tests must be performed quarterly
- Recovery Time Objective (RTO): 4 hours for critical systems
- Recovery Point Objective (RPO): 24 hours for critical systems

6. THIRD-PARTY SECURITY POLICY

6.1 Vendor Assessment
All third-party vendors with access to company data or systems must:
- Complete annual security questionnaire
- Provide SOC 2 Type II or equivalent certification
- Undergo security assessment before contract execution
- Comply with all applicable security policies

6.2 Data Processing Agreements
- Data Processing Agreements (DPA) required for all vendors processing customer data
- Vendors must notify company of security incidents within 24 hours
- Right to audit vendor security controls upon request

7. SECURITY AWARENESS AND TRAINING

7.1 Training Requirements
- Annual security awareness training mandatory for all employees
- Specialized training required for IT and security staff
- Phishing simulation exercises conducted quarterly
- Training completion rate must be 100% within 30 days of hire or annual deadline

8. COMPLIANCE AND AUDIT

8.1 Policy Compliance
- Policy compliance assessments conducted annually
- Non-compliance incidents tracked and reported to management
- Repeat policy violations may result in disciplinary action

8.2 Audit Requirements
- Internal security audits conducted semi-annually
- External security audits conducted annually
- Audit findings must be remediated within 90 days

9. POLICY GOVERNANCE

9.1 Policy Review
This policy must be reviewed and updated annually or when significant changes occur.

9.2 Approval Authority
Policy changes require approval from:
- Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)
- Chief Information Officer (CIO)
- Legal Counsel
- Executive Management

9.3 Exceptions
Policy exceptions require written approval from CISO and must be reviewed quarterly.
