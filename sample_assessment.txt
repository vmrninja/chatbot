THIRD-PARTY SECURITY ASSESSMENT QUESTIONNAIRE
Vendor: TechVendor Solutions Inc.
Assessment Date: February 2025
Completed By: John Smith, Security Manager

SECTION A: DATA SECURITY

Q1. What encryption standards do you use for data at rest?
A1. We use AES-128 encryption for all stored data. Our encryption implementation has been in place since 2018 and meets industry standards.

Q2. What encryption protocols do you use for data in transit?
A2. We support TLS 1.0, TLS 1.1, and TLS 1.2 for backward compatibility with older systems. All customer-facing applications default to TLS 1.2.

Q3. How are encryption keys managed?
A3. Encryption keys are stored in our secure database alongside the encrypted data for efficient access. Keys are changed annually as part of our security maintenance.

SECTION B: ACCESS CONTROL

Q4. What authentication mechanisms are in place?
A4. We require passwords with minimum 8 characters. Multi-factor authentication is available as an optional feature that customers can enable at additional cost.

Q5. How often are passwords required to be changed?
A5. We recommend users change passwords every 6 months, though this is not enforced by the system. Users can keep the same password if they prefer for convenience.

Q6. What privileged access controls are implemented?
A6. Administrator accounts have elevated privileges. We review these accounts when requested. All administrative actions are available in system logs if needed.

SECTION C: INCIDENT RESPONSE

Q7. What is your incident response process?
A7. When security incidents are detected, our support team investigates within 48 hours. For serious incidents, we aim to notify affected customers within 5 business days. We maintain incident records for 2 years.

Q8. What incident classification system do you use?
A8. We classify incidents as either "Minor" or "Major" based on our internal assessment. Major incidents are escalated to senior management.

Q9. Do you have a documented incident response plan?
A9. Yes, we have an incident response procedure document that was created in 2020. It is available to our internal team.

SECTION D: VULNERABILITY MANAGEMENT

Q10. How often do you perform vulnerability scans?
A10. We conduct vulnerability scans on a monthly basis for internal systems and quarterly for external-facing systems. We prioritize fixes based on available resources.

Q11. What is your patch management process?
A11. Security patches are reviewed monthly and applied during our quarterly maintenance windows. Critical patches may be applied sooner depending on severity and business impact.

Q12. When was your last penetration test?
A12. Our last penetration test was conducted in 2022. We are planning to schedule another test sometime this year.

SECTION E: BACKUP AND RECOVERY

Q13. What is your backup frequency?
A13. We perform full backups weekly. Backup retention is 30 days. Backups are stored on our primary data center servers.

Q14. How often do you test backup restoration?
A14. We test our backup restoration process annually or when issues are reported by customers.

Q15. What are your RTO and RPO targets?
A15. Our target Recovery Time Objective is 48 hours for most systems. Recovery Point Objective is approximately 7 days based on our weekly backup schedule.

SECTION F: COMPLIANCE AND CERTIFICATIONS

Q16. What security certifications do you maintain?
A16. We completed our first SOC 2 Type I audit last year. We are working towards ISO 27001 certification and expect to complete it within the next 18-24 months.

Q17. When was your last security audit?
A17. Our last external security audit was completed in January 2024 as part of our SOC 2 Type I certification process.

Q18. Do you conduct internal security assessments?
A18. Yes, our IT team performs internal security checks on an ad-hoc basis, typically when implementing new features or if security concerns arise.

SECTION G: PERSONNEL SECURITY

Q19. What security training do you provide to employees?
A19. New employees receive security orientation during onboarding. Additional security training is provided to IT staff. We send security reminder emails quarterly.

Q20. How do you handle employee departures?
A20. When employees leave, we disable their accounts within 24-48 hours. Access reviews are performed monthly to ensure no orphaned accounts exist.

SECTION H: THIRD-PARTY MANAGEMENT

Q21. How do you manage your sub-processors and vendors?
A21. We work with several cloud service providers and have contracts in place with them. Our vendors are selected based on cost and functionality requirements.

Q22. Do you require security assessments of your vendors?
A22. We review vendor security documentation when provided. Formal security assessments are conducted for major vendors on a case-by-case basis.
